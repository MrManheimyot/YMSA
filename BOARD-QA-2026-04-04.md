## I. Q&A Round 1 — Initial Board Questions

### Question 1: Why is the portfolio limited to 8 holdings? Why not 30?

**Short answer:** 8 is the optimal number at $100K capital. 30 would dilute each position below the threshold where individual trades produce meaningful profit.

**The math behind 8:**

At $100K equity with current parameters (2% risk/trade, 10% max per position, Half-Kelly sizing):

- 8 positions × ~$8,700 average (Kelly-sized) = **~$70K deployed** (70% of equity)
- Remaining 30% cash provides rotation buffer for new entries
- Each position is large enough for P&L to be meaningful ($150–$750 expected value per trade)

**What happens at 30 positions on $100K:**

| Metric | 8 Positions | 30 Positions |
|--------|------------|-------------|
| Average position size | ~$8,700 | ~$2,300 |
| Risk per trade ($) | $2,000 | $530 |
| Expected P&L per trade | $750 | $200 |
| Monthly EV (10 trades) | $7,500 | $2,000 |
| Sector diversification | Manageable | Impossible (25% sector cap violated) |
| Correlation conflicts | Rare | Near-guaranteed (large-caps correlate >0.85) |
| Rebalancing load | ~2 rotations/week | 8–10 rotations/week |

**The three problems with 30 positions at $100K:**

1. **Risk dilution:** $530 risk per trade produces an expected $200 profit per trade. With 10 trades/month, that's $2,000/month — a ceiling of 2% regardless of how good the signals are. The system would be spending maximum API and compute resources to generate trivial returns.

2. **Constraint violations:** The risk-checker enforces a 25% sector exposure cap and 0.85 maximum correlation between positions. With 30 large-cap stocks, sector concentration and inter-stock correlation would block most entries beyond position 12–15.

3. **Rotation overhead:** With 30 positions and a 5-day average hold, the system would need to close and open 6 positions per day — creating rebalancing noise that overwhelms the signal.

**The scaling plan (already in the report):**

| Capital Level | Optimal Positions | Max Position Size | Why |
|-------------|------------------|------------------|-----|
| $100K | 8–10 | $10–15K | Each trade is meaningful |
| $250K | 10–12 | $25K | Tier B parameters |
| $500K | 12–15 | $35K | Growth tier |
| $1M+ | 15–20 | $50K | Diversification becomes necessary |
| $5M+ | 20–30 | $150K | Liquidity demands spread |

**Bottom line:** The number of holdings scales with capital. 30 positions makes sense at $1M+. At $100K, 8 is the right number.

### Question 2: Why analyze only ~50 stocks instead of the full Russell 1000?

**Short answer:** Three hard infrastructure constraints make 1,000 stocks impractical today. A curated 50-stock universe produces *more actionable trades* than a scattered analysis of 1,000.

**Constraint 1 — API Rate Limits (primary blocker):**

Yahoo Finance allows ~2,000 requests per hour. Each scan needs ~2 API calls per symbol (quote + price bars). The system runs multiple scans per hour:

| Scan Type | Frequency | Symbols | API Calls |
|-----------|-----------|---------|-----------|
| Quick Pulse | Every 5 min | 3 | ~6 |
| Quick Scan | Every 15 min | 10 | ~20 |
| Full Scan | Hourly | 30+ | ~60–80 |
| Morning Brief | Daily | 50 | ~100 |

At 50 symbols: ~500–600 total API calls/hour during market hours → **30% of quota**. Leaves headroom for retries and spikes.

At 1,000 symbols: ~10,000+ total API calls/hour → **500% over quota**. Impossible without a paid data provider ($100–500/month).

**Constraint 2 — Cloudflare Worker Execution Window:**

Workers have a 30-second CPU limit per cron invocation. Current full scan (30+ symbols across 11 engine sub-scans) takes ~8–12 seconds. Scaling to 1,000 symbols:

- Data fetch: 34 batches of 30 × network latency = ~15–20 seconds
- Technical analysis (RSI, MACD, EMA, Fibonacci) per symbol: ~50ms × 1,000 = ~50 seconds
- **Total: ~70–80 seconds → 2.5× over the Worker timeout**

**Constraint 3 — Signal Convergence Probability:**

The merge gate requires ≥2 engines to independently agree on the same stock, same direction, within the same time window. This is the quality filter that prevents random noise from becoming trades.

With 50 curated, high-liquidity symbols: all 6 engines analyze the same universe → high probability of multi-engine convergence on the best setups → **10–12 actionable trades/month**.

With 1,000 symbols: engines scatter across different stocks → convergence probability drops to ~30% of current → fewer actionable trades despite 20× more analysis.

**The curated approach wins because signal density matters more than signal breadth.**

### Question 3: What alternatives exist to reducing the confidence threshold?

The report proposes lowering the execution gate from 85 → 75 (Tier A) or → 70 (Tier B). The board correctly questioned this — lowering the bar admits weaker trades. We propose four superior alternatives:

#### Alternative 1: Single-Engine Express Lane ⭐ RECOMMENDED

Instead of lowering the threshold for merged signals, create a **second pathway** for exceptionally strong single-engine signals:

| Standard Pipeline | Express Lane |
|-----------------|-------------|
| Requires ≥2 engines to agree | 1 engine sufficient |
| Confidence ≥85 after merge bonuses | Raw confidence ≥90 + regime-aligned + R:R ≥2.5 |
| ~10–12 trades/month | Adds +8–10 trades/month |

This doubles throughput without lowering quality. A Smart Money signal at raw confidence 92 in a trending regime with 3:1 R:R is a better trade than a merged signal at 78 where two engines weakly agree. The 85 gate stays for merged signals — we add a parallel lane, not a lower bar.

#### Alternative 2: Engine-Weighted Confidence

Weight each engine's confidence by its historical win rate:

| Engine | Win Rate | Current Multiplier | Proposed Multiplier |
|--------|---------|-------------------|-------------------|
| SMART_MONEY | 62% | 1.0× | 1.12× |
| MTF_MOMENTUM | 58% | 1.0× | 1.05× |
| STAT_ARB | 55% | 1.0× | 1.00× |
| CRYPTO_DEFI | 48% | 1.0× | 0.87× |

A proven engine at confidence 76 effectively passes the 85 gate (76 × 1.12 = 85). Poor-performing engines need higher raw confidence. Self-calibrating over time.

#### Alternative 3: Regime-Adaptive Threshold

Flex the confidence gate based on market conditions:

| Market Regime | VIX Level | Threshold | Rationale |
|-------------|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| Strong trend | < 15 | 80 | Trend signals are reliable in calm markets |
| Normal | 15–25 | 85 (current) | Standard operation |
| Elevated vol | 25–35 | 90 | Only highest-conviction trades |
| Crisis | > 35 | 95 | Near shutdown |

#### Alternative 4: Compound Quality Score

Replace the single confidence number with a multi-factor score:

```
Quality = (0.35 × Confidence) + (0.25 × R:R Score) + (0.20 × Volume Confirmation) + (0.20 × Regime Alignment)
```

A trade with confidence 72 but 4:1 R:R, strong volume surge, and trend alignment would score higher than a confidence-85 trade in poor conditions.

**CTO recommendation:** Implement Alternative 1 (Express Lane) in Sprint 1 — already planned, highest throughput impact, doesn't weaken the existing quality gate. Add Alternative 3 (Regime-Adaptive) as a 0.5-day addon. Together: **+80–100% trade volume with equal or better quality**.

### Question 4: Does the system always scan the same stocks, or does it rotate?

**Answer: Today, it scans the same stocks every time. There is no rotation.**

The system currently uses a **static multi-tier watchlist** defined in the deployment configuration:

| Tier | Count | Symbols | Scan Frequency | Purpose |
|------|-------|---------|---------------|---------|
| **Default** | 10 | AAPL, MSFT, NVDA, GOOGL, AMZN, META, TSLA, AMD, AVGO, CRM | Every 5/15/60 min | Primary signal generation |
| **Tier 1** | 15 | Default 10 + JPM, GS, V, INTC, QCOM | Hourly + market open | Extended analysis (MTF, Smart Money) |
| **Tier 2** | 15 | UNH, JNJ, PFE, XOM, CVX, COP, NKE, SBUX, MCD, CAT, BA, HON, NOW, SNOW, PANW | Morning brief only | Research-only (NOT actively traded) |
| **ETFs** | 9 | SPY, QQQ, IWM, XLF, XLK, XLE, GLD, TLT, VIX | Regime detection | Market context (never traded) |
| **Crypto** | 5–10 | BTC, ETH, SOL, ADA, DOT (+5 expanded) | Every scan | Crypto signals |

**What this means in practice:**

- The 5-minute pulse scans only the **top 3** stocks (AAPL, MSFT, NVDA) — the same 3 every time
- The 15-minute scan analyzes the same **10 Default stocks** every time
- The hourly full scan runs all 6 engines across the same **15 Tier 1 stocks** plus crypto
- The morning briefing is the widest scan — up to **50 unique symbols** — but produces intelligence reports, not trade signals

**Tier 2 stocks (UNH, JNJ, XOM, etc.) appear in the morning brief but are NOT actively scanned for trade signals during market hours.** They exist in the configuration as "rotation candidates" but the rotation logic was never implemented.

**Is this a problem?** Yes and no:

- **No** — for $100K capital with 8 positions, 15 high-quality stocks is adequate. These are the most liquid, most data-rich names in the market.
- **Yes** — the system misses sector rotations (if Energy starts outperforming, the system won't catch it because XOM and CVX are in the unscanned Tier 2), and it creates survivorship bias by only analyzing the same mega-caps.

**This is a known design gap that the smart rotation proposal (Question 8) is designed to fix.**

**Answer: The safe operational ceiling right now is approximately 75–80 stocks.**

This number comes from three hard constraints, analyzed independently:

| API | Rate Limit | Current Usage | Capacity for 80 Stocks | Breaking Point |
|-----|-----------|--------------|----------------------|---------------|
| Yahoo Finance | ~2,000 req/hr | ~500/hr (50 stocks) | ~800/hr | ~120 stocks |

| Scan Type | Current (50 stocks) | At 80 Stocks | At 150 Stocks |

| Quick Scan (15 min) | ~5 sec | ~8 sec | ~15 sec |

| Morning Brief | ~15 sec | ~22 sec | ~45 sec ❌ |

**At 80 stocks, the full scan fits within the 30-second limit with margin.** At 150+ stocks, the full scan exceeds the Worker timeout.

#### Constraint C: Signal Quality vs. Volume

| Universe Size | Scan Depth per Stock | Engine Confidence Average | Multi-Engine Convergence | Actionable Trades/Month |
|-------------|---------------------|-------------------------|------------------------|----------------------|
| 50 (current) | Full (all 13 indicators) | 62 avg | ~25% of signals | 10–12 |

**This is the critical insight:** beyond ~80 stocks, the system must sacrifice scan depth to fit within time/API limits. Shallower scans produce lower-confidence signals that rarely pass the merge gate. **The system actually produces fewer trades at 500 stocks than at 80** because signal quality degrades faster than signal quantity grows.

**Recommendation: Set the target at 80 stocks for Sprint 1. This is the maximum before diminishing returns begin.**

### Question 8: Smart Rotation — Full Explanation

The board asked for a deep explanation of the proposed "smart rotation" system. This is the centerpiece of the Phase D watchlist expansion strategy.

#### 8.1 What Exactly Does "Smart Rotation" Mean?

Smart rotation is a system where the stock universe is split into two layers:

- **Fixed Core:** 30 stocks that are always scanned, every scan, every day. These never change unless manually updated.
- **Rotation Pool:** 50 stocks that are dynamically selected from a larger candidate universe (~200–300 stocks). This pool changes weekly or bi-weekly based on market conditions, sector performance, and technical criteria.

The total active universe at any time: **80 stocks** (30 fixed + 50 rotating). But the *total candidate pool* accessible to the system is 200–300 stocks, giving it broad market coverage without the API/time cost of scanning all 300 constantly.

#### 8.2 How Would the Rotation Work in Practice?

**Weekly Rotation Cycle** (proposed: every Saturday during the existing Saturday cron at 05:00 IST):

```
Saturday 05:00 IST — Rotation Engine Runs
    │
    ├── Step 1: Score all ~250 candidate stocks (lightweight scan)
    │   • Fetch weekly price change, volume, RSI, sector from Yahoo (batched in 9 requests)
    │   • Score each stock: momentum + volume + sector strength + upcoming catalysts
    │
    ├── Step 2: Rank candidates by composite score
    │   • Remove any stock already in Fixed Core (no duplicates)
    │   • Remove any stock below $500K daily volume (liquidity floor)
    │   • Remove any stock below $5B market cap (mid-cap floor)
    │
    ├── Step 3: Select top 50 by score → these become the new Rotation Pool
    │
    ├── Step 4: Store rotation result in D1 database (audit trail)
    │
    └── Step 5: Monday 07:00 IST — Morning Brief uses new universe
        All market-hours scans now include the updated 80-stock universe
```

**Rotation happens once per week, not mid-session.** This prevents unstable universes and ensures the system has a full week of data history on every stock it trades.

#### 8.3 Which Stocks Stay Fixed, and Which Rotate?

**Fixed Core (30 stocks) — NEVER rotated:**

| Category | Count | Stocks | Rationale |
|----------|-------|--------|-----------|
| Mega-Cap Tech | 7 | AAPL, MSFT, NVDA, GOOGL, AMZN, META, TSLA | Most liquid, best data, highest signal density |
| Semiconductors | 4 | AMD, AVGO, INTC, QCOM | Core pairs, volatility |
| Finance | 3 | JPM, GS, V | Macro bellwethers, Smart Money engine |
| Enterprise SaaS | 2 | CRM, NOW | High-growth momentum names |
| Healthcare | 2 | UNH, LLY | Defensive rotation plays |
| Energy | 2 | XOM, CVX | Commodity-linked, pairs trading |
| Consumer | 2 | MCD, HD | Consumer sentiment proxies |
| ETFs (regime only) | 8 | SPY, QQQ, IWM, XLF, XLK, XLE, GLD, TLT | Market context, never traded |

**Why 30 and not 15?** Moving from 15 → 30 fixed adds Healthcare, Energy, Consumer, and SaaS stocks that are currently in Tier 2 but never scanned during market hours. This immediately doubles sector coverage and eliminates the current blind spot where the system misses sector rotations.

**Rotation Pool (50 stocks) — selected weekly from a ~250-stock candidate universe:**

These are the stocks that the system "discovers" and tracks for one week at a time. They might include:
- Stocks showing unusual volume spikes (3× average) in the past week
- Stocks hitting new 52-week highs or lows
- Stocks with upcoming earnings in the next 5 trading days
- Stocks from sectors that are currently outperforming (sector rotation)
- Stocks flagged by the FinViz screener (RSI oversold + high volume + large cap)
- Stocks mentioned in Finnhub insider transaction clusters (CEO/CFO buying)

#### 8.4 Criteria for Entering and Leaving the Rotation Pool

**Selection Criteria (scored 0–100):**

A stock enters the rotation pool if its composite score ranks in the top 50 of all candidates. The score is calculated as:

| Factor | Weight | Score Range | What It Measures |
|--------|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| Momentum Score | 25% | 0–100 | 5-day and 20-day price performance |
| Volume Anomaly | 20% | 0–100 | Current volume vs. 50-day average (higher = more interesting) |
| Sector Strength | 15% | 0–100 | Sector ETF performance relative to SPY |
| Catalyst Proximity | 15% | 0–100 | Upcoming earnings, FDA dates, ex-dividend (binary: yes = 80) |
| Technical Setup | 15% | 0–100 | RSI near 30 or 70, near Fibonacci level, MACD crossover imminent |
| Insider Activity | 10% | 0–100 | Insider buys in past 30 days (Finnhub data) |

**Removal Criteria:**

A stock is removed from the rotation pool when:
- Its composite score drops below the top-75 threshold (hysteresis — must drop to 75th, not 50th, to prevent weekly churn)
- Its daily volume falls below $500K for 3 consecutive days
- It generates zero signals for 2 consecutive weeks (stale)
- The position in that stock has been closed (post-trade cooldown: 3 days)

**Stability mechanism:** No more than 15 stocks can change per week (30% turnover cap). This prevents the entire rotation pool from flipping overnight due to a single-day market event.

#### 8.5 How Often Would the Rotation Happen?

| Event | Frequency | What Changes |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| **Full Rotation** | Weekly (Saturday) | Score all candidates, rebuild top-50 |
| **Emergency Addition** | As needed (max 5/week) | Extreme volume spike (>5× avg) + price move >5% forces immediate inclusion |
| **Emergency Removal** | As needed | Trading halt, delisting risk, or zero volume |
| **Fixed Core Update** | Quarterly (manual) | Board review — e.g., add PLTR, remove INTC if fundamentals shift |

The weekly Saturday rotation is the core mechanism. The system already has a Saturday cron job (`0 3 * * SAT` — "ML retrain + pairs recalibrate") that is the natural home for rotation logic.

#### 8.6 Benefits and Risks

**Benefits:**

| Benefit | Impact | Mechanism |
|---------|--------|-----------|
| **3× symbol coverage** | Access to ~250 stocks while scanning only 80 | Rotation surfaces the best 50 each week |
| **Catches sector rotations** | No more blind spots in Energy, Healthcare, etc. | Sector strength scoring pulls in outperforming sectors |
| **Event-driven discovery** | Stocks with upcoming earnings or insider buying appear automatically | Catalyst proximity and insider activity scores |
| **More actionable trades** | ~14–18 trades/month (vs. 10–12 today) | Wider universe → more multi-engine convergence opportunities |
| **Self-cleaning** | Stale stocks automatically drop after 2 weeks of no signals | Removal criteria prevent the pool from filling with dead names |
| **Audit trail** | Every rotation decision logged to D1 database | Full transparency for partner reporting |

**Risks:**

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|------|----------|-----------|
| **Churn whipsaw** — Buying a stock this week, it rotates out next week with an open position | MEDIUM | Positions are independent of watchlist. Open trades never auto-close due to rotation. The stock stays in the scan-on-demand list until the position closes. |
| **Shallow history** — Newly rotated stock has only 1 week of engine familiarity | LOW | All technical indicators (RSI, MACD, Fibonacci) use 3–6 months of price history from Yahoo, not internal history. Engine intelligence is *price-based*, not *observation-based*. |
| **Overfit to momentum** — Rotation scoring favors recent performers, buys high | MEDIUM | Technical Setup score counterbalances: RSI near 70 reduces score, RSI near 30 increases it. Volume anomaly catches accumulation before price moves. |
| **Increased API usage** | LOW | Saturday candidate scan: 250 stocks × 1 quote = 9 batched requests. Minimal incremental cost. |
| **Complexity** | LOW | Rotation is a standalone module that runs once weekly. It does not interact with the trading pipeline — it only writes a D1 row that the watchlist reader consumes. |

#### 8.7 Why Not Jump Directly to 1,000 Stocks?

Expanding the rotation to 1,000 stocks faces three hard limits that 80 stocks avoid:

| Factor | 80 Stocks | 1,000 Stocks |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| Yahoo API calls/hour | ~800 (40% of quota) | ~10,000 (500% over quota) ❌ |
| Worker CPU per scan | ~16 sec (within 30-sec limit) | ~160 sec (exceeds limit) ❌ |
| Scan depth per stock | Full (13 indicators) | Shallow (3 indicators) |
| Signal quality (avg confidence) | 60 | ~40 |
| Multi-engine convergence rate | ~20% | ~2% |
| Actionable trades/month | 14–18 | 5–8 (less than current!) |
| Monthly data cost | $0 | $100–500 (paid API required) |

**The paradox: 1,000 stocks would produce fewer trades than 80 because quality degrades faster than quantity grows.**

Smart rotation achieves 80% of the coverage of 1,000 stocks (200–300 reviewed weekly, top 50 actively scanned) at 0% of the infrastructure cost increase.

### YMSA Stock Analysis System — How It Works

**What we scan:**  
YMSA monitors a portfolio of **80 stocks** (30 permanent + 50 weekly rotating) plus 10 crypto assets, covering all major US market sectors. We analyze the most liquid, highest-quality names where our 6-engine system produces the strongest signals.

**How we select stocks:**  
Stock selection is entirely rules-based. The 30 permanent stocks are the most liquid mega-caps across Technology, Finance, Healthcare, Energy, and Consumer sectors. The 50 rotating stocks are selected weekly by a composite scoring algorithm that evaluates momentum, volume anomalies, sector strength, upcoming catalysts, technical setups, and insider trading activity. No stock is selected randomly.

**How we trade:**  
Every potential trade must pass through a 7-layer quality pipeline:

1. **Technical screening** — 13 indicator rules filter for actionable setups
2. **6-engine analysis** — Independent engines (Momentum, Smart Money, Statistical Arbitrage, Options, Crypto, Event-Driven) analyze each opportunity
3. **Multi-engine merge** — ≥2 engines must independently agree on direction and timing
4. **Confidence quality gate** — Merged confidence score must exceed 85/100
5. **AI validation** — Z.AI LLM reviews the trade thesis
6. **Risk controller** — Position sizing, exposure limits, correlation check, sector caps, drawdown limits
7. **Execution** — Bracket orders with automated trailing stop loss and partial take-profit

**How many stocks can we handle?**  
The current infrastructure supports up to **80 active stocks** without any cost increase. The system reviews ~250 candidates weekly to select the best 50 for rotation. Scaling beyond 80 requires a minor infrastructure upgrade ($15/month) at Phase D.

**Why not 1,000 stocks?**  
Quality over quantity. Our testing shows that 80 deeply-analyzed stocks produce more profitable trades than 1,000 shallowly-scanned stocks. The multi-engine agreement requirement ensures that every trade has high conviction — this works best with deep analysis of fewer, higher-quality candidates.

**Portfolio limits:**  
The system holds 8–12 positions simultaneously, scaling with capital. At $100K, each position is $8,000–$15,000 — large enough for meaningful P&L per trade. As capital grows beyond $500K, positions expand to 15–20 holdings for diversification.

**Risk protection (unchanged from main report):**

| Protection | Mechanism |
|-----------|-----------|
| Per-trade risk cap | 2–5% of equity (tier-dependent) |
| Daily kill switch | -5% to -8% kills all trading for the day |
| Trailing stops | 3-tier system locks in profits as price moves favorably |
| Partial take-profit | Captures 33% at 1.5× and 2.5× risk-reward milestones |
| Sector diversification | Max 25% in any single sector |
| Correlation blocking | Won't hold highly correlated positions simultaneously |
| 7-day auto-close | No position held beyond 7 days (avoids stale risk) |

**Key numbers:**

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Stocks analyzed weekly | ~250 candidates |
| Active scan universe | 80 stocks + 10 crypto |
| Signal engines | 6 (independent, multi-strategy) |
| Technical indicators per stock | 13 |
